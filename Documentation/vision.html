Business Requirements

Background

For our bachelor subject, Informatics Engineering Project, we were given a pool of project themes to choose from and work on. Given the options, our team opted to go for a project that could have an impact on the daily life of someone in need and hopefully be helpful. We named our project, Goodbye CVA. As the name self-explains, Goodbye CVA focus on the cognitive rehabilitation of post-stroke patients, helping them overcome all mental disabilities created by the pathology.

Business Opportunity

With Goodbye CVA we have a platform that provides cognitive exercises with relatable content for each patient (favourite hobbies/activities, family pictures, etc). This comes to fill a gap that none of the existing platforms do, since they provide standardized task/exercise templates that don’t capture the patient’s attention and fail to motivate.

Business Goals

  • Development of a fully functioning prototype, with which we can test with real patients and evaluate areas of improvement to our platform
  • Provide meaningful and well designed tasks/exercises templates, with the possibility of customization from the doctor
  • Provide doctors the access to task results and statistics for each patient

Vision Declaration

Our platform will be designed with ease of use in mind due to the demographic it will be used by (people in some way incapacitated), allowing patients to focus on the tasks.

Products and Similar Projects

There are similar products available, such as NEP-UM and Metrisquare.

As we do in our project, NEP-UM also focuses on cognitive rehabilitation, in areas such as memory, focus, language and executive functions. It provides assignment of sessions and specific tasks for the patients and monitoring of patient’s performance.

Metrisquare focuses on cognitive training (not rehabilitation). It allows the creation of different tasks using standardized templates.

Stakeholder Profiles

We have two kinds of stakeholder profiles affected by our business activity.

  1. Primary Stakeholder: Doctors
  2. Market stakeholder: Patient
  1. Doctors
    • Improve work productivity
    • Reduced rework and waste
    • Costs savings
    • Automation of previously manual tasks
    • Ability to perform entirely new tasks
    • Improved usability compared to current products
  2. Patiets
    • Improve work productivity
    • Automation of previously manual tasks
    • Ability to perform entirely new tasks
    • Improved usability compared to current products
    • Chance to practice with relatable content

Business Risks

  • Short schedule for completion of the project
  • Stakeholders acceptance. Patients may not enjoy working on the platform and/or doctors may not feel that our platform is a fitting replacement to their current practices.

Business Dependencies and Other Considerations

We will offer task templates, but we assume doctors will expend time to make them relatable for each patient, since we believe that is one of the strong points of our platform.

Needs and Features

  • Customization (doctors can customize tasks)
  • Versatility (tasks can be adapted)
  • Monitoring (doctors can monitor patients progress and task results)
  • Practicality (patients can do tasks at home)
  • Relatability (patients will have tasks with relatable content)
