Client Area

    Our platform “Stroke Rehab” has two actors: doctors and patients. It has authentication for both of them and the main objective of this platform is to have various templates/tasks for the patients so they can evolve their mental disabilities.

    The templates must be different due to the differentiated stroke levels of the patients and the doctors can decide what templates and how many templates must a patient perform. Those templates are made by the doctors (configured by them) in order to create tasks for each patient and create a “patient story”. For example, in “Multiple Choice Template”, the doctor can configure a question about something that is related to the patient’s life or even in “Nominate Template”, the doctor can configure an image of a patient’s relative. This configuration side is very important because it has much more impact on the client and the patient’s recovery would be easier and faster. Also, it is easier for the doctors to connect with the platform and patients, creating various templates.

    Other objective is that the doctors can save the templates and set them for the patients they want to perform it. They can edit them later and schedule for a date one or more templates for the patients. One advantage is that the templates have feedback phrases, in order to don’t let the patient give up or, if the patient succeeded in the task, to congratulate him. The doctors can create or edit those feedback phrases in a more appropriate way of each patient.

    Another main goal of the platform is that have statistic charts of the task’s results. According to the results, each patient have a page on his account that have his results and the doctors can see their improvements and then, keep or change his tasks program.

    The templates that the platform has are: “Nominate Task”, “Sequence Task”, “Multiple Choice Task”, “Categorization Task” and “Visual Recognition Memory Task”. In “Nominate Task”, the patient sees a photo of someone that is his relative or friend and tries to discover his name by some hidden words. In “Sequence Task”, the patient have multiple images to be drag and dropped into some boxes down below the images. In “Multiple Choice Task”, the patient have one question and a possible of 2 or more answers and have to guess what is the correct one. In “Categorization Task”, the patient have multiple images to be drag and dropped into some boxes down below the images. In “Visual Recognition Memory Task”, the patient have multiple images and he has to select the ones that are related to the category shown below the images.

    Due to the fact some patients have more disabilities than others, if a patient can’t do one task by himself he can do it with the help of the doctor. If the patient can’t point with one finger to the computer screen, maybe he can speak and do the task in the same. There are many disabilities in Stroke Rehab and they can be voided replacing with other features. That’s why we added the voice recognition functionality. We added it in templates so that the patient can speak and try to do the task anyway. For example, in “Nominate Task” the patient can try to guess the name of the person that is in the photo and say it. If the name is wrong, it appears a wrong feedback message, if it is correct, appears a correct feedback message.

Specification Area

    Our platform will have two actors: doctors and patients. Both of them will have authentication and different types of accounts.

    The patient’s account will have their info data, their pre-defined/scheduled templates and their results of the tasks in charts. It will be available in different pages and this can be all accessible by the doctors. The patients can also choose what templates they want to do or to do firstly, or else the doctors can choose them randomly.

    The doctor’s account will have one page for the templates/tasks configuration, to save, edit and delete them, they can define and schedule the templates for each patient and see the statistics of the results of the tasks done by the patients. They can also add, edit and remove patients to the database, simultaneously giving each of them an username and a password to sign in later in order to complete the tasks previously configured.

Templates

Nominate Template:

    In this task, the patient sees a photo of someone that is his relative or friend and tries to discover his name by some hidden words. When he clicks on the black box that misses one letter, he (or the doctor if he can’t do it by himself) clicks again on the letter that is associated to that hidden letter. If he wrongs, appears a message that tries to motivate him and not let him giving up. If he hits, appears a feedback message that encourage to conclude it and saying that he is doing great.

Sequence Template:

In this task, the patient have multiple images to be drag and dropped into some boxes down below the images. If he can’t do it by himself, the doctor must do it for him by his choice of answer. If he drag and drop one image to the wrong box he gets a message saying to try again. If he gets all the correct boxes, he receives a feedback message saying that he has successfully done the task. The images can be numbers, daily sequence tasks or anything that the doctors think that are good to get the patients’ recover.

Multiple Choice Template:

In this task, the patient have one question and a possible of 2 or more answers and have to guess what is the correct one. The questions may be whatever the doctors choose for the respective doctors. May be daily questions or questions about their families or their personal life. If the patient hits he receives a feedback message that says the answer is correct. If he gets the wrong answer the patient has more tries to guess and he receives a message to not give up.

Categorization Template:

In this task, the patient have multiple images to be drag and dropped into some boxes down below the images. The images must be dropped into the box that is the same category of the image. If he can’t do it by himself, the doctor must do it for him by his choice of answer. If he drag and drop one image to the wrong box he gets a message saying to try again. If he gets all the correct boxes, he receives a feedback message saying that he has successfully done the task. The images can be anything that the doctors think that are good to get the patients’ recover.

Visual Recognition Memory Template:

In this task, the patient have multiple images and he has to select the ones that are related to the category shown below the images. If he can’t do it by himself, the doctor must do it for him by his choice of answer. If he selects one wrong image he gets a message saying to try again. If he gets all the correct images, he receives a feedback message saying that he has successfully done the task. The images can be related to the patients’ family or their personal life, daily tasks to improve their daily abilities or anything that the doctors think that are good to get the patients’ recover.

Multiple Choice Template with Virtual Reality:

In this task, the patient have one question and a possible of 2 or more answers and have to guess what is the correct one (the same of Multiple Choice Task). Using a Virtual Reality glasses, the patient can move his head around and he can sees a little point in the middle of the screen glasses. He has to aim that point to the correct answer box. If the patient aims to the correct box, the box turns green and he receives a correct feedback message, if he aims to one of the wrong answers, the box turns red and the patient has more tries to guess.

Nominate Template with Virtual Reality:

In this task, using virtual reality glasses, the patient has to aim the middle point of the screen to the empty boxes. When he selects one empty box, then he points to the letter he thinks that is correct. If the patient aims to the correct box, the box gets the corrected letter and he receives a correct feedback message, if not he gets a wrong feedback message and have more tries to correct it.

Multiple Choice Template with Leap:

In this task, the patient have one question and a possible of 2 or more answers and have to guess what is the correct one (the same of Multiple Choice Task). Using a Leap device, the patient can point his finger to one answer on the computer screen. He has to point and stay 2 seconds on the box he thinks that is correct. If the patient aims to the correct box, the box turns green and he receives a correct feedback message, if he aims to one of the wrong answers, the box turns red and the patient has more tries to guess.
