Purpose

    The purpose of this document is to provide an overview on the Stroke Rehab platform. Giving insight on the purpose and features of the system, as well as its goal and constraints.

General Description

    Stroke Rehab is a platform for cognitive rehabilitation for post-stroke patients. It will make use of a simple but productivity driven user interface. Making the life of doctors and patients easier and the experience of rehabilitation more engaging.

    The platform will focus on customization, configuration and adaptability, filling a gap in the current market which focuses on generic, non-personal tasks.

System Features

    ● FE-01 - User authentication

    ● FE-02 - Doctor area

    ● FE-03 - Patient area

For Doctors:

    ● FE-04 - Patient management

    ● FE-05 - Task customization and assignment area

    ● FE-06 - Patient progress monitorization area

For Patients:

    ● FE-07 - Simple interaction

    ● FE-08 - “Ready to go” tasks

    ● FE-09 - Positive feedback

Functional Requirements

    ● The platform must be simple and easy to use as well as visually appealing

    ● Tasks must be easy to understand and to work with

    ● Monitoring area must provide important and easily understandable information

    ● Make good use of authentication to provide privacy of information

Client Area

    We already have some functional templates and they are ready to be tested. As the client faces many disabilities created by the Stroke pathology, the tasks must be easy to use, with non-distractful functionalities and user-friendly.

    At this moment, we created two sides of templates. The first one is the patient side, where is a working demo that can be released to the neuropsychologists and to be tested with the patients. There we can receive some reviews and feedback, and some possible corrections about them. The other side is the doctor side, where the doctors can create their own templates using a base template. It is implemented a simple template (user-friendly and ease to use) that doctors can create templates to the various among patients in the clinic. Due to the different disabilities that patients have, doctors must create different tasks with different levels to help their recovers and make their improvements much better.

    Here we can show the functional templates that are ready to be delivered to the neuropsychologists and be tested:

Nominate Task

In this task, the patient sees a photo of someone that is his relative or friend and tries to discover his name by some hidden words. When he clicks on the black box that misses one letter, he (or the doctor if he can’t do it by himself) clicks again on the letter that is associated to that hidden letter. If he wrongs, appears a message that tries to motivate him and not let him giving up. If he hits, appears a feedback message that encourage to conclude it and saying that he is doing great.

Sequence Task

In this task, the patient have multiple images to be drag and dropped into some boxes down below the images. If he can’t do it by himself, the doctor must do it for him by his choice of answer. If he drag and drop one image to the wrong box he gets a message saying to try again. If he gets all the correct boxes, he receives a feedback message saying that he has successfully done the task. The images can be numbers, daily sequence tasks or anything that the doctors think that are good to get the patients’ recover.

Multiple Choice Task

In this task, the patient have one question and a possible of 2, 3 or 4 answers and have to guess what is the correct one. The questions may be whatever the doctors choose for the respective doctors. May be daily questions or questions about their families or their personal life. If the patient hits he receives a feedback message that says the answer is correct. If he gets the wrong answer the patient has more tries to guess and he receives a message to not give up.

Categorization Task

In this task, the patient have multiple images to be drag and dropped into some boxes down below the images. The images must be dropped into the box that is the same category of the image. If he can’t do it by himself, the doctor must do it for him by his choice of answer. If he drag and drop one image to the wrong box he gets a message saying to try again. If he gets all the correct boxes, he receives a feedback message saying that he has successfully done the task. The images can be anything that the doctors think that are good to get the patients’ recover.

Visual Recognition Memory Task

In this task, the patient have multiple images and he has to select the ones that are related to the category shown below the images. If he can’t do it by himself, the doctor must do it for him by his choice of answer. If he selects one wrong image he gets a message saying to try again. If he gets all the correct images, he receives a feedback message saying that he has successfully done the task. The images can be related to the patients’ family or their personal life, daily tasks to improve their daily abilities or anything that the doctors think that are good to get the patients’ recover.

Developer Area

    he templates/tasks being tested on the patients are created in Java Script / JQuery to facilitate the adaption to the platforms that we are going to use, such as in browser in personal computers, laptops, tablets or even mobile phones.

    The platform that we can implement “all together-in-one” is called “Firebase” and we already have the authentication and the security entity functionalities.
